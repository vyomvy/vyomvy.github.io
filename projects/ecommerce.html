Pipeline 02 / E-commerce

E-commerce user conversion analysis

Merged 6 raw tables into one funnel view to find where users were actually dropping off — and who was really driving revenue.

Live dashboard ↗ Source code ↗
The problem

An e-commerce dataset spread across 6 separate CSV tables — users, sessions, carts, orders — with no unified view of how visitors actually moved toward a purchase, or where the funnel was leaking.

The approach
01
Merge the tables

Joined 6 CSVs covering 56,000+ orders and 10,000+ users into one analyzable dataset with Pandas.

02
Map the funnel

Traced the full user journey from view to cart to purchase to find the biggest drop-off point.

03
Visualize with Matplotlib

Built charts to communicate signup timing, funnel drop-off, and revenue concentration clearly.

Key findings
63%

Of users complete a purchase before ever signing up — a strong checkout-first behavior pattern.

View→Cart

The single biggest drop-off point in the entire funnel — the moment most users disengage.

Top 10%

Of customers drive a disproportionate share of revenue — a concentration risk worth flagging.
