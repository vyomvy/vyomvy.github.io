KEY FINDINGS

Six patterns hiding in 1,500 orders

Each finding below came from a specific SQL query — distance buckets, refund-rate grouping, and time-of-day segmentation — then verified visually in Power BI.

Risk

First-time users refund the most

First-time orders show the highest refund rate of any segment — more consistent with account-level discount abuse than genuine delivery failure. New accounts may be created specifically to claim first-order offers.

Risk

Distance breaks service quality

Refund rate climbs steadily with distance and hits ~17% beyond 9km. Average rating falls in step — the further the order travels, the more likely something goes wrong before it arrives.

Opportunity

Long-distance orders pay the most

Despite the highest refund risk, orders beyond 9km generate the highest average order value of any distance bucket — the riskiest orders are also the most profitable ones.

Opportunity

Mahanagar leads, but narrowly

Mahanagar is the top-performing area by revenue — but its order mix is dominated by a single item (rolls). One restaurant category is carrying an entire area's revenue.

Insight

Daytime owns the order volume

Every one of the 10 areas studied places more orders in the day (6 AM – 6 PM) than at night — demand is not a late-night-snacking story here, it's a daytime habit.

Insight

Discounting is uneven across the menu

Some items run discount rates well above the platform average, quietly eating into margin on specific categories rather than spread evenly across the menu.

HOW IT WAS BUILT

One pipeline, three tools, one job each

No tool does everything here. Python only cleans, SQL only answers business questions, Power BI only tells the story.

01 / PYTHON

Clean

Raw export loaded in Pandas on Colab. Job ends at producing one trustworthy CSV — no analysis happens here.

  • Classified nulls as structural vs. real (e.g. no rating ≠ missing data)
  • Fixed sign errors in distance_km
  • Standardised text casing across area & food_item
  • Removed duplicate order rows
02 / SQL

Answer

Cleaned CSV loaded into MySQL. Every business question answered as its own query — CTEs and window functions, not Pandas.

  • Distance buckets vs. delivery time & order value
  • Discount % by food item
  • Refund rate by order type
  • Day vs. night demand by area
03 / POWER BI

Present

Same five questions, rebuilt as an interactive dashboard — KPIs, slicers, and charts a non-technical stakeholder can use unsupervised.

  • 4 top-line KPI cards
  • Area & order-type slicers
  • 6 linked visuals, one per business question
